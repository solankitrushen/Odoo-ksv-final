# PRODUCT.md — Rental Customer Portal

register: brand+product (marketing pages are brand; catalog/cart/account are product)

## Product purpose

The customer-facing website for a rental management system. People come here to
browse physical rental inventory (equipment, event gear, tools, furniture, cameras),
choose a rental period, reserve with a security deposit, and manage their orders,
returns, and refunds. It is the storefront and self-service portal that sits in
front of the admin/back-office rental operation.

## Users

- **Renters (portal users):** individuals and small businesses renting physical goods
  for a defined period. They care about: what's available, for which dates, how much
  (rate + deposit), when to pick up / return, and getting their deposit back.
- Mixed technical literacy. Mobile-first is common. Trust and clarity matter more
  than density: this involves their money (deposits) and deadlines (late fees).

## Brand & tone

Editorial, precise, quietly confident. A well-run rental house, not a loud
marketplace. Copy is plain and direct: states rates, periods, deposit terms, and
return deadlines without hype. Never salesy, never uses exclamation points.

## Strategic principles

1. **Money and time are always legible.** Rate, rental period, security deposit,
   pickup/return dates, and any late-fee exposure are never buried. The customer
   should never be surprised by a charge.
2. **The deposit is a promise, not a fee.** Framing throughout: the deposit is held
   and returned. Late returns reduce it by a stated penalty. This is shown honestly.
3. **Reservation over transaction.** Renting is booking a window of time, not buying.
   The period picker is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.

## Anti-references

- Not a neon marketplace (no aggressive discounts, urgency timers, or clutter).
- Not generic SaaS-cream with a purple gradient and rounded hero-metric cards.
- Not a food-delivery clone despite the sibling codebase.

## Register palette (mandated)

Near-monochrome, 60/30/10: ~60% near-white surface, ~30% near-black ink,
~10% neutral gray as the working accent. Differentiation comes from typography,
whitespace, and layout rhythm, not from color.
